Executive Summary

The Challenge

  • Manual bid and budget adjustments consumed hours every week.
  • Spend frequently overshot targets on higher-volume days.
  • A lean team had little time left for creative and strategy.

The Solution

  • Connected Google and Meta into a single Shirofune workspace.
  • Enabled AI auto-budgeting with MinROAS targets per group.
  • Adopted smart recommendations to prune wasted spend daily.

The Results

  • ROAS improved 38% within the first quarter.
  • Hands-on management time dropped by roughly 60%.
  • Budgets stayed on target every month, with no overspend.

CRIO BRU is a direct-to-consumer brand crafting roasted and ground cacao as a caffeine-free coffee alternative. Its small in-house marketing team runs paid acquisition across search and social to fuel subscription growth.

Full Story

Outgrowing manual optimization

As CRIO BRU scaled its subscription business, the daily work of managing bids and budgets across Google Ads and Meta grew faster than the team could keep up with. Every platform had its own quirks, and reconciling spend against monthly targets meant constant manual checks.

The team wanted to keep tight control of profitability without hiring a dedicated media buyer — and without letting each ad platform's black-box automation push spend higher than the numbers justified.

Putting a human-like algorithm to work

With Shirofune, CRIO BRU connected both channels into one workspace and set MinROAS targets at the budget-group level. Shirofune's auto-budgeting then reallocated spend toward the best-performing campaigns each day — the same judgment a seasoned manager would apply, executed automatically.

  • Daily budgets and target ROAS adjusted without manual intervention.
  • Smart recommendations flagged non-converting keywords and audiences to pause.
  • One-click cross-channel reports replaced hours of spreadsheet work.

Shirofune gave us back most of our week. We set the guardrails once, and it manages the day-to-day like a specialist would — without ever pushing us to overspend.

Compounding results

Within the first quarter, return on ad spend rose 38% while hands-on management time fell by roughly 60%. Just as importantly, budgets stayed on target every month, giving leadership confidence to reinvest the reclaimed hours into creative testing and lifecycle marketing.
